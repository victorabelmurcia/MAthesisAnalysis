Codebook

for: DataLab202.csv

Main info:

DataLab202.csv contains data on individual respondnts. Data on places that the respondents visit is stored separate files.

The dataset is arranged according to the rules of tidy data, so every row is an observation and every columns is a variable. This is also the reason why data on places is stored elsewhere - according to the rules of tidy data each type of information should have its own dataset.

The dataset contains various demographic and socio-economic variables. Moreover there is a lot of information on cultural preferences and habits as well as social/civic activity, social capital (operationalized according to the Resource Generator model) place attachment towards Warsaw.

Dimensions

  • Rows (respondents): 202
  • Columns (variables): 193

Variables:

id

Id number of the respodnent. Necessary for correct dataset merging.

gender

Respondent's gender

Values:

  • woman
  • man

birth

Date of birth

marital

Marital status

Values:

  • single
  • nf_rel - non-formal stable relationship of any kind
  • married

Wwa

Living in Warsaw - it was used as a filtering variable

district

District of Warsaw in which a respondent lives

timeWwa

The amount of time a respondent has been living in Warsaw

children

Children (yes/no)

dog

Dog (yes/no)

edu

Respondent's education

Values:

  • <=highschool+tech - education that equals or is lower than high school or technical school
  • BA - bachelor
  • MA+ - MA or higher

eduprog

My own classification of the type of education obtained by a respondent

Values:

  • human/lang - humanities or language studies
  • law/biz/menag - law school, bussiness studies/economy/finance or menagement studies of various kinds (i.e. MBA)
  • leisure - leisure studies and related disciplines
  • STEM/med - STEM (Science, Technology, Engeneering, Math) or medical/biological science
  • soc/beh - social and behavioral sciences

edutime

Whether a respondent is attending a full-time educaton right now (yes/no)

work

Does a respondent currently have a job (yes/no)

worktime

Working hours

Values:

  • no_job
  • part - part-time job
  • flexible - flexible not regulated working time
  • full - full-time job

occup

Type of occupation. This variable has many categories that are quite sparse. Thus the variable is rather useless

ISCO

Occupational classification loosely based on ISCO-08. Also very sparse and very useful

ISCObroad

Much more general and broad occupational classification drived from ISCO.

Values:

  • low:psych/sale/serv/assoc_pro - lower tier jobs. Clerical, physcal, mid-level technicians, sales and service, interns, etc.
  • high:mng/pro/self - higher tier jobs. Managers, self-employment, professionals, high-skilled technicians, scientists etc.

fatheredu

Father education. The same value labels as in edu except for an addition of "I don't know".

motheredu

Mother education. The same value labels as in edu except for an addition of "I don't know"

grandedu

Education of the most educated grandparent. The same value labels as in edu except for an addition of "I don't know"

income

Monthly income in (x, x+1) thousands (pln). Bounded from above by 10, what means that 10 indicates 10+ thousands of income, so the scale looses reliability in the case of extreme observations. However, there are very few such observations in the dataset.

lifestd

Perceived standard of life. Quantitative description that ranges from 1 to 5.

cars

Number of a respondent's cars

Values:

  • no_car
  • 1car+ - one or more cars

hometype

Domicile type

Values:

  • apartment
  • other

homestatus

Homeownership status of the domicile

Values:

  • rent
  • own
  • family - respondent's home belongs to his/her family
  • other

tv

Does a respondent have a TV? (yes/no)

pc

Does a respondent have a personal computer (portable or not)? (yes/no)

tablet

Does a respondent have a tablet? (yes/no)

internet

How often does a respondnt use the internet. There is no variablity in this question - everyone uses it everyday

internetuse

Does a respondent have his own blog/website?

Values:

  • no_blog/web
  • blog/web

Social / civic activity battery: socact1 to socact14

Answers: (yes / no)

First part: did you do any of these during the last 12 months?

  1. Signing a petition
  2. Organizing a protest
  3. Participating in a protest / demonstration
  4. Undertaking an action (alone or in a group) in order to influence your local area
  5. Undertaking an action (alone or in a group) in order to influence your work place or a place of education
  6. Volunteering

Second part: Are you an active memebr of any of the following organizations?

  1. Political party
  2. Local government
  3. NGOs
  4. Local organizations / school organization / neighbourhood organization
  5. Trade union
  6. Parish / religious organization
  7. Hobby related organization
  8. Sport club
